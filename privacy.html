Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

The short version: Nearly everything Audex stores lives on your device or in your own Google Drive account. One small exception: a minimal server we operate verifies when your account first started a free trial, so it can't be reset by reinstalling. That server never sees your files, listening activity, or any personal details — see below. We don't sell, share, or analyze your data, and we don't show ads.

What Audex accesses, and why

Google Sign-In

Audex uses Google Sign-In to identify your account. It stores your email address and display name in encrypted local storage on your device.

Google Drive — folder import (read-only)

If you choose to import an audio folder from Google Drive, Audex requests read-only access to browse and download the files you select. It never accesses any other file in your Drive, and never writes to your regular, visible Drive space.

Google Drive — app data folder

Audex uses Google's hidden, per-app appDataFolder space (invisible in your regular Drive, and inaccessible to any other app) for an optional, user-controlled backup of your local playback history, resume positions, and settings, so a phone change or reinstall doesn't lose them. This lives entirely inside your own Google account — we never see or receive it ourselves.

Trial verification (anti-piracy)

To keep the free trial honest without collecting anything about you, Audex sends the ID token Google already issues at sign-in to a small server we operate (Cloudflare Workers). That server verifies the token's signature directly against Google's own public keys, then stores exactly two things against your account's opaque Google ID: a single "first seen" timestamp, and (after purchase) a confirmation that you've unlocked the app. It never receives your email, name, files, listening activity, or any other personal data - just enough to know "this account has been trialing Audex since date X," so reinstalling or clearing app data can't reset the clock. This check runs once per install, right after sign-in.

Local device storage

Audex stores your playback history, resume positions, settings, downloaded/synthesized audio, and any books you import directly on your device, using Android's standard local database and file storage. This never leaves your device unless you explicitly use the backup feature above.

Google Play Billing

Purchases are handled entirely by Google Play. Audex never collects or sees your payment details (card number, billing address, etc.) — Google Play does. Audex only receives a purchase confirmation from Google's own systems.

Feedback (bug reports / feature requests)

Audex's "Report a bug" and "Request a feature" buttons open your own email app with a pre-filled draft addressed to the developer. Audex does not send anything itself — nothing leaves your device unless you personally choose to tap Send in your own email app.

What Audex does not do

  • No analytics or usage-tracking SDKs of any kind
  • No advertising, no ad SDKs
  • No crash-reporting or telemetry sent to a third party
  • No selling or sharing of your data with anyone

Data deletion

Uninstalling Audex removes everything stored locally on your device. To remove the Drive-stored backup, revoke Audex's access from your Google Account's third-party access settings, which deletes its entire appDataFolder space. To remove the trial-verification record described above, contact us at the email below and we'll delete it.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will change accordingly. Material changes will also be reflected in the app's own release notes.
